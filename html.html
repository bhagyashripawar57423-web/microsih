About Microplastics

Quick reference for judges and reviewers

Microplastics are plastic particles smaller than 5 mm. They appear as fibers, fragments, pellets and microbeads and come from many sources — clothing, packaging, cosmetics and industrial processes. They can harm ecosystems and enter the food chain.

  • Types: Fibers, Fragments, Pellets, Microbeads
  • Impacts: ingestion by organisms, transport of pollutants
  • How this system works: image input → (model analyzes) → outputs size/type/accuracy → visual comparisons & history
